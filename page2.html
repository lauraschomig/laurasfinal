Climate Change Disproportiontely Affects Minorities
Building climate change resistance homes, acquiring adequate insurance/health care and relocating to less vulnerable areas is unaffordable for low income populations. This leads to:
  • Greater exposure to climate hazards
  • Greater susceptibility to damages caused by climate hazards
  • Less ability to cope with and recover from climate related damages.
  • Disproportionate loss of assets for low income populations.
The Effect of Climate Change in US
American Indians, Alaskan Natives, and Latinos are over 40% more likely to live in communities vulnerable to climate change. U.S. experiences 8,500 excess deaths of mostly minority citizens due to climate change.
  • Impoverished residents lack resources to climate change side effects (winds, flooding, hurricanes, lightening)
  • Expected to rise to over 100,000 excess deaths by 2050
